Cold Lentil Salad

Description

This lentil salad is very simple and yet very tasty. It is perfect for picnics and summer gatherings. If you grow fresh herbs and tomatoes, it will come out even better. It is appropiate for vegan, gluten-free, and generally healthy eating.

Ingredients

  • 2 bulbs of garlic
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil, divided
  • 1 pound dry brown lentils
  • 4 cups of water, or as needed to cover
  • 1 pint grape tomatoes, halved
  • Fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • Green onions, thinly sliced
  • ¼ cup balsamic vinegar
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).

  2. Peel outer skins from the garlic bulbs and cut about 1/4 inch from the top of each bulb to expose cloves; place each bulb onto a piece of aluminum foil, drizzle each bulb with 1/2 teaspoon of olive oil, wrap garlic bulbs in the foil. Place wrapped bulbs into a baking dish.

  3. Bake the garlic in the preheated oven until soft, for about 30 minutes. Once done, let the bulbs cool down.

  4. Place the lentils into a 5-quart pot and cover with water; bring the water to a boil. Reduce the heat to medium-low and let the lentils simmer until they're tender, but not mushy, between 15 to 18 minutes; stir occasionally. Once done, drain the lentils in a fine-mesh strainer and rinse with cold water to cool; drain again.

  5. Break the garlic bulbs apart and squeeze the cloves to release the cooked garlic. Place garlic into a large salad bowl and add the grape tomatoes, parsley, and green onions. Whisk balsamic vinegar with 1/4 cup of olive oil, salt, and black pepper in a small bowl. Fold the lentils into the mixture and pour dressing over the salad; gently toss to coat. Keep refrigerated until serving time.
